## Risk Rating Methodology

**Likelihood (1–5):**

| Score | Rating | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Rare | Highly unlikely; no known precedent |
| 2 | Unlikely | Possible but not expected |
| 3 | Possible | May occur; some industry precedent |
| 4 | Likely | Probable; occurs regularly in similar organisations |
| 5 | Almost Certain | Expected; occurs frequently |

**Impact (1–5):**

| Score | Rating | Description |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| 1 | Negligible | Minimal disruption; no regulatory consequence |
| 2 | Minor | Limited disruption; internal impact only |
| 3 | Moderate | Significant disruption; possible regulatory exposure |
| 4 | Major | Serious disruption; likely regulatory or reputational harm |
| 5 | Catastrophic | Business-threatening; severe consequences |

**Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact**

| Score | Risk Level |
|-------|-----------|
| 1–4 | Low |
| 5–9 | Medium |
| 10–15 | High |
| 16–25 | Critical |

## Treatment Options Reference

| Option | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Treat** | Implement controls to reduce likelihood or impact to an acceptable level |
| **Tolerate** | Accept the risk as within the organisation's risk appetite; monitor |
| **Transfer** | Transfer risk via insurance or contractual arrangement (e.g., with a supplier) |
| **Terminate** | Cease the activity that creates the risk |
