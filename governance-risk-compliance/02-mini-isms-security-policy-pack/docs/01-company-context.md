# 01 - Company Context

## Company background

BrightBox Retail Ltd is a small UK online retail business with around 15 employees. It sells consumer products through a Shopify storefront and supports customers through email, phone, and order management tools.

The company has no dedicated security team. Security tasks are shared between the director, operations manager, staff, and an external IT support provider.

## Business operations

The main business activities are:

- online sales and order processing
- customer support
- supplier and stock management
- finance and payment reconciliation
- marketing and website content updates
- fulfilment coordination

Most work depends on cloud services and staff laptops. The office network supports day-to-day operations but does not host major internal systems.

## Technology environment

| Area | Current environment |
|------|---------------------|
| Email and collaboration | Microsoft 365 |
| Online sales | Shopify |
| File storage | Cloud file storage linked to Microsoft 365 |
| End-user devices | Windows laptops |
| Business email | Named staff mailboxes and some shared mailboxes |
| Office connectivity | Small office network and Wi-Fi |
| IT support | External IT support provider |

## Data handled

BrightBox handles:

- customer names, addresses, email addresses, and phone numbers
- order details and delivery information
- payment transaction status from Shopify/payment providers
- supplier contact details
- employee records and payroll-related information
- business finance and operational records

Full card payment data is handled by payment providers and should not be stored in company systems.

## Key security concerns

The main concerns are:

- phishing and account takeover
- weak access controls on shared mailboxes and cloud files
- poor joiner, mover, and leaver processes
- laptop loss or malware infection
- inconsistent data handling
- limited backup and restore testing
- supplier access and third-party dependency
- lack of a clear incident reporting process

## Reason for creating the policy pack

The company needs a basic structure for assigning security responsibilities, setting minimum rules, recording key assets and risks, and reviewing controls over time. The policy pack is designed to be realistic for a small company and practical enough for staff and managers to use.
