# 05 - Asset and Risk Overview

## Asset overview

BrightBox relies on a small number of cloud services, laptops, shared accounts, and operational records. The most important assets are customer data, Microsoft 365 accounts, Shopify admin access, staff laptops, business email, and backup records.

The detailed asset list is in the [asset register](../registers/asset-register.md).

## Key information assets

| Asset area | Why it matters |
|------------|----------------|
| Customer data | Needed for orders, delivery, support, and data protection obligations. |
| Microsoft 365 | Supports email, files, identity, collaboration, and shared mailboxes. |
| Shopify | Supports online sales and order processing. |
| Staff laptops | Used to access most company systems and data. |
| Business records | Needed for finance, supplier management, and operations. |

## Main risks

The main risks are listed in the [risk register](../registers/risk-register.md). They include phishing, account takeover, poor access removal, laptop loss, cloud file oversharing, weak backup testing, supplier risk, and limited incident response.

## How risks influence policy requirements

The policy pack is risk-led. For example:

- phishing and account takeover drive MFA, password, and awareness requirements
- joiner/mover/leaver risk drives access control and review requirements
- customer data risk drives data classification and handling rules
- ransomware risk drives backup and restore testing requirements
- supplier dependency drives supplier review and access rules
- incident handling risk drives reporting, escalation, and review requirements

This keeps the policies linked to practical business risk rather than abstract control lists.
