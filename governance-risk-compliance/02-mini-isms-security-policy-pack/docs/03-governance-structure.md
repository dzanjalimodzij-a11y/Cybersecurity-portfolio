# 03 - Governance Structure

## Governance model

BrightBox uses a simple governance model with clear ownership. The director is accountable for security decisions, the operations manager coordinates day-to-day activity, the IT support provider implements technical controls, and staff follow the policies.

## Role responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| Director | Approves policies, accepts or rejects risk treatment decisions, reviews security performance, and assigns budget where needed. |
| Operations Manager | Maintains policy records, coordinates access reviews, tracks incidents, and follows up on improvement actions. |
| IT Support Provider | Implements technical controls, supports MFA and device configuration, maintains backups, and advises on technical risk. |
| Staff | Follow policies, protect devices and accounts, handle data correctly, and report security concerns quickly. |
| Suppliers | Protect BrightBox data and systems they access, follow agreed security requirements, and report relevant issues. |

## Escalation path

1. Staff report security issues to the operations manager.
2. The operations manager records the issue and contacts the IT support provider where technical help is needed.
3. The director is informed of incidents that may affect customer data, business operations, finance, or reputation.
4. Serious incidents are reviewed by the director, operations manager, and IT support provider.

## Policy approval

Policies are drafted by the operations manager with input from the IT support provider. The director approves each policy before it is issued to staff.

## Policy review

Policies are reviewed at least annually. Reviews may happen sooner after incidents, major system changes, supplier changes, or legal/regulatory updates.

## Management review

The director should review the ISMS at least once a year. The review should cover open risks, incidents, access review results, backup test results, supplier issues, staff training, and planned improvements.
