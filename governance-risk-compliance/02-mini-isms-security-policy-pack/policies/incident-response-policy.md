# Incident Response Policy

## Purpose

This policy defines how BrightBox staff should report and handle security incidents.

## Scope

This policy applies to suspected or confirmed incidents affecting company systems, devices, accounts, suppliers, or information.

## What counts as an incident

Examples include:

- suspected phishing or account compromise
- lost or stolen laptop or phone
- malware or ransomware alert
- customer data sent to the wrong recipient
- unauthorised access to Shopify, Microsoft 365, or cloud storage
- supplier security issue affecting BrightBox data
- unexpected outage affecting business operations

## Policy requirements

- Staff must report suspected incidents to the operations manager as soon as possible.
- The operations manager must record the incident and contact the IT support provider when technical support is needed.
- Initial containment may include password resets, account disablement, device isolation, or removal of sharing links.
- The director must be informed where customer data, finance, business operations, or reputation may be affected.
- Evidence such as emails, logs, screenshots, and affected files should be preserved where possible.
- A post-incident review must be completed for significant incidents.

## Roles and responsibilities

| Role | Responsibilities |
|------|------------------|
| Director | Makes business and regulatory decisions for significant incidents. |
| Operations Manager | Coordinates reporting, recording, escalation, and review. |
| IT Support Provider | Supports containment, investigation, and recovery. |
| Staff | Report issues quickly and preserve relevant information. |

## Evidence or records

- incident log
- containment actions
- communications record
- post-incident review

## Review frequency

This policy should be reviewed annually or after a significant incident.
