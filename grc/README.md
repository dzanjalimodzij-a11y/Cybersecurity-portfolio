# GRC Projects

This section documents Governance, Risk and Compliance (GRC) projects, demonstrating the ability to apply structured risk management and information security governance frameworks in practical scenarios. These projects are aligned with internationally recognised standards including ISO/IEC 27001 and the NIST Cybersecurity Framework.

---

## Projects

### 1. [Cyber Risk Assessment — Small UK Online Tutoring Business](./risk-assessment-small-business/)

A complete cyber risk assessment for BrightPath Tutoring Ltd, a small UK online tutoring business that handles children's personal data, online payments, and a public WordPress site. It covers company profiling and scope, a documented risk-scoring methodology, an asset register, a threat register, a 10-risk risk register, prioritised control recommendations, and a NIST CSF mapping — finished with an executive summary and conclusion for non-technical stakeholders.

**Key skills:** Scope definition, risk methodology design, asset & threat identification, risk register creation, control selection (technical/procedural/physical), framework mapping (NIST CSF 2.0 / ISO 27001), UK GDPR & PCI DSS awareness, executive communication.

---

### 2. [Mini ISMS Policy Pack](./mini-isms-policy-pack/)

A set of six core information security policies aligned to ISO 27001 requirements, along with a risk treatment plan, control mapping, and implementation roadmap. Demonstrates the ability to create professional, enforceable security policies suitable for a small to medium-sized organisation.

**Key skills:** Policy writing, ISO 27001 alignment, risk treatment planning, implementation roadmap development, control framework mapping.

## Why GRC?

Technical security controls are only effective when supported by sound governance, clear policies, and systematic risk management. GRC skills are essential for security roles in compliance-sensitive industries, management-track positions, and any organisation required to demonstrate regulatory or contractual security requirements. These projects show the ability to think strategically about security — not just technically.
