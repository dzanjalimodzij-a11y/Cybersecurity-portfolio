# EV-005 - Incident Log Example

## Evidence ID

EV-005

## Evidence title

Low-risk incident log entry

## Related policy or register

[Incident Response Policy](../policies/incident-response-policy.md)

## Control area

Incident reporting and follow-up

## Example record details

| Field | Example entry |
|-------|---------------|
| Incident summary | Suspected phishing email sent to shared customer service mailbox |
| Date reported | 2026-06-18 |
| Reported by | Customer Service Adviser |
| Initial action taken | Email forwarded to IT support provider and deleted from shared mailbox |
| Containment | Sender blocked and similar messages searched in Microsoft 365 |
| Outcome | No link clicked and no credentials entered |
| Lessons learned | Remind staff not to use shared mailbox credentials and to report suspicious links |
| Owner | Operations Manager |

## Why this evidence is important

Incident logs show that staff are reporting issues and that the company is taking follow-up action. Even low-risk events are useful for spotting patterns and improving awareness.

## Review or collection frequency

Record incidents as they happen and review the log at least quarterly.

## Notes on limitations

This example does not include technical email headers, screenshots, or mail trace exports. A live record should attach relevant technical evidence where available.
