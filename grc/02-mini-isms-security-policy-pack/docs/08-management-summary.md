# 08 - Management Summary

## Summary

A mini ISMS and policy pack has been created for BrightBox Retail Ltd. It gives the company a practical structure for managing security responsibilities, policies, assets, risks, suppliers, incidents, and review activity.

## What has been created

- an ISMS scope and company context
- a simple governance structure
- ten short security policies
- asset, risk, policy, and responsibility registers
- a control mapping against practical governance outcomes
- a review and improvement plan
- an evidence plan for future records and screenshots

## Main governance improvements

The policy pack gives BrightBox:

- clearer ownership of security decisions
- consistent rules for accounts, devices, data, backups, and incidents
- a record of key assets and risks
- a route for policy approval and review
- a basic evidence trail for security activity
- a more structured approach to supplier and staff responsibilities

## Remaining limitations

The pack is a starting point. It does not prove that controls are fully implemented, tested, or operating effectively. Technical configuration evidence, staff acknowledgements, access review records, and backup test results would need to be collected over time.

## Recommended next steps

1. Approve the policy pack.
2. Confirm MFA for Microsoft 365, Shopify, finance systems, and admin accounts.
3. Complete an initial access review.
4. Test a backup restore and record the result.
5. Ask staff to acknowledge the core policies.
6. Start a basic incident log and supplier review record.
7. Review the risk register at the next management meeting.

## Conclusion

BrightBox can improve its security governance without creating a heavy process. The priority is to make ownership clear, keep the policies usable, and collect enough evidence to show that basic controls are being maintained.
