# 02 - ISMS Scope

## Scope statement

The mini ISMS covers the people, processes, systems, and information used to operate BrightBox Retail Ltd's online retail business. It focuses on protecting customer information, business records, staff devices, cloud services, and the core processes that support sales, fulfilment, and customer service.

## In scope

### Teams and roles

- director and management responsibilities
- operations and customer service staff
- finance and administration users
- marketing users with access to Shopify and shared files
- external IT support provider activities that affect company systems

### Systems

- Microsoft 365 accounts, email, shared mailboxes, Teams, OneDrive, and SharePoint
- Shopify storefront and admin access
- cloud file storage
- Windows laptops used for company work
- office Wi-Fi and basic network equipment
- security tools used for malware protection, MFA, logging, and backup

### Data

- customer contact and delivery information
- order and transaction records
- supplier records
- employee information
- finance and business records
- policy and governance documentation

### Processes

- user account creation, changes, and removal
- access reviews
- device security
- data classification and handling
- backups and restore testing
- incident reporting and response
- supplier security review
- staff security awareness

## Out of scope

- payment provider internal systems
- Shopify internal platform security
- supplier internal networks
- personal systems not used for company work
- detailed penetration testing
- formal ISO 27001 certification planning

Third-party services remain in scope only where BrightBox controls account settings, access permissions, data sharing, contracts, or supplier review activity.

## Scope justification

The scope is based on the systems and processes that carry the highest business and data protection risk. For a small retailer, the most practical starting point is to protect accounts, devices, customer data, cloud storage, backups, suppliers, and incident reporting.

## Assumptions and limitations

- BrightBox has limited internal IT knowledge and relies on an external provider for technical changes.
- The company can implement basic security controls but cannot maintain complex enterprise processes.
- Policies should be short enough for staff to understand and follow.
- Evidence collection should be lightweight and linked to normal business activity.
