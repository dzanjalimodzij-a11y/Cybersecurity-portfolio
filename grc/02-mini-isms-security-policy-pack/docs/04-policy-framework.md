# 04 - Policy Framework

## How the policies fit together

The policy pack sets minimum security expectations for BrightBox. The information security policy gives the overall direction. The supporting policies explain the specific rules for accounts, devices, data, backups, suppliers, remote working, incidents, and staff awareness.

## Policy ownership

| Policy area | Owner |
|-------------|-------|
| Information security policy | Director |
| Acceptable use and remote working | Operations Manager |
| Access control, passwords, MFA, and backups | IT Support Provider with Operations Manager oversight |
| Data handling and supplier security | Operations Manager |
| Incident response | Operations Manager and IT Support Provider |
| Security awareness | Operations Manager |

## Review cycle

All policies should be reviewed annually. High-risk policies, such as access control, backups, and incident response, should also be reviewed after relevant incidents or major system changes.

## Staff acknowledgement

Staff should confirm that they have read the core policies during induction and after major updates. A simple acknowledgement record is enough for a small company.

## Policy exceptions

Policy exceptions must be recorded with:

- the policy requirement affected
- the reason for the exception
- the risk created
- the person approving the exception
- the review date

The director must approve exceptions that affect customer data, finance systems, admin access, or backups.

## Policy breaches

Suspected breaches should be reported to the operations manager. The response should consider the impact, whether customer or employee data is affected, and whether training or disciplinary action is needed. The aim is to correct behaviour and reduce risk, not to punish honest mistakes.
