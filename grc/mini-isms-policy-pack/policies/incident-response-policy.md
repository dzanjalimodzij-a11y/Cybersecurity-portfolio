## 1. Purpose

To ensure that information security incidents are identified, reported, and managed in a consistent and effective manner that minimises harm to the organisation, its clients, and its staff, and meets applicable regulatory requirements.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all staff, contractors, and third parties who use company systems or handle company data.

## 3. What Is a Security Incident?

A security incident is any event that has — or could have — an adverse impact on the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of company information or systems.

**Examples include:**
- Suspected phishing email or malware infection
- Loss or theft of a device containing company data
- Unauthorised access to systems or data
- Accidental disclosure of client or personal data
- Ransomware or other destructive malware
- Successful account compromise

Incidents also include "near misses" — events that did not cause harm but could have.

## 4. Reporting Responsibilities

**All staff must:**
- Report any suspected or confirmed security incident as soon as possible
- Report to: [PLACEHOLDER — IT Manager / Security Contact / Helpdesk]
- Contact method: [PLACEHOLDER — Phone / email / Teams message]
- Not attempt to investigate or resolve the incident themselves (unless specifically trained to do so)

**There is no penalty for reporting in good faith.** Early reporting is critical to minimising harm.

## 5. Incident Response Process

### Phase 1: Identification and Reporting

- Incident identified by staff member, system alert, or third party
- Report made to IT Manager / Security Lead
- Incident logged in the incident register with date, time, description, and reporter details
- Initial severity assessed

### Phase 2: Triage and Classification

| Severity | Criteria | Initial Response Time |
|----------|---------|----------------------|
| Critical | Ransomware / active breach / major data loss | Immediate |
| High | Account compromise / significant data exposure | Within 2 hours |
| Medium | Suspected phishing / minor data incident | Within 4 hours |
| Low | Policy violation / near miss | Next business day |

### Phase 3: Containment

Actions to prevent the incident from spreading or causing further harm, e.g.:
- Isolating an affected device from the network
- Suspending a compromised user account
- Blocking a malicious email sender
- Preserving evidence

### Phase 4: Eradication and Recovery

Removing the root cause and restoring normal operations safely.

### Phase 5: Post-Incident Review

A review must be conducted within 10 business days of incident closure to identify lessons learned and improvements to controls or procedures.

## 6. Regulatory Notification

Where an incident involves personal data, the IT Manager and Managing Director must assess whether the incident constitutes a reportable personal data breach under UK GDPR. If so:

- The ICO must be notified within **72 hours** of becoming aware of the breach (where feasible)
- Affected data subjects must be notified without undue delay if the breach is likely to result in high risk to their rights and freedoms

Legal advice should be sought in the event of a significant breach.

## 7. Records

All incidents must be recorded in the incident register, including:
- Incident description and timeline
- Severity classification
- Actions taken at each phase
- Outcome and lessons learned

Incident records must be retained for a minimum of [3 years / in line with legal requirements].
