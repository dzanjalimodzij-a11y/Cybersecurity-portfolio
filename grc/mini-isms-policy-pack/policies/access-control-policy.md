## 1. Purpose

To ensure that access to company systems, applications, and data is granted on a need-to-know, least-privilege basis, and that access rights are managed throughout the user lifecycle.

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all access to company information systems and data, including cloud services, on-premises systems, and physical access to secure areas.

## 3. Access Control Principles

All access decisions must be based on the following principles:

- **Least privilege:** Users are granted only the access rights necessary to perform their role.
- **Need to know:** Access to sensitive data is restricted to those with a legitimate business need.
- **Separation of duties:** Where feasible, critical functions are divided between multiple individuals to reduce fraud and error risk.
- **Default deny:** Access is denied by default; it must be explicitly granted and justified.

## 4. User Access Lifecycle

### 4.1 Access Provisioning (Joiner)

When a new member of staff or contractor joins:
1. Line manager submits an access request to IT Manager, specifying required systems and access levels
2. IT Manager creates accounts and grants access in accordance with the role profile
3. User is informed of their access rights and directed to this policy and relevant training

### 4.2 Access Changes (Mover)

When a staff member changes role:
1. Line manager notifies IT Manager of the change and required access modifications
2. IT Manager reviews existing access, removes access no longer required, and adds new access within 5 business days
3. Changes are logged

### 4.3 Access Removal (Leaver)

When a staff member leaves:
1. HR notifies IT Manager on (or before) the staff member's last working day
2. All system access is disabled on the last working day or earlier if required
3. Company devices are recovered
4. Access is fully removed within 30 days

## 5. Privileged Access

- Privileged accounts (administrator access) must be separate from standard user accounts
- Privileged access must not be used for day-to-day tasks (e.g., email, web browsing)
- A register of all privileged accounts and their justification must be maintained
- Privileged access must be reviewed quarterly

## 6. Access Reviews

All user access rights must be formally reviewed:
- **All users:** Annually
- **Privileged accounts:** Quarterly
- **Following a security incident:** As required

Reviews must be documented. Any access that cannot be justified must be removed.

## 7. Remote Access

Remote access to company systems is permitted only via the approved VPN solution and must be protected by MFA. Refer to the Acceptable Use Policy for remote working requirements.

## 8. Third-Party Access

Third-party access to company systems must be:
- Formally requested and approved by a Director
- Time-limited to the duration of the requirement
- Subject to the same controls as internal user access
- Removed promptly on contract completion
